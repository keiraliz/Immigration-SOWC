Inmigration is moving from one's native country to another.

A lot of the time when people have to flee the country to another they do so illegally. That's why now the term inmigration has such a bad rep socially. It is no more than people going from one place to another but due to the immense illegal inmigration that's been going on for so long in so many countries it has become the term of a social problem that almost every country faces.

There are a lot of reasons someone would want to immigrate elsewhere. The most common ones are war, educational purposes, poverty, because of natural disasters, unemployment, violation of human rights and many others.


War

Due to the war between Russia and Ukraine there is an estimated of 6.6 millions refugees spread through Poland, Romania, Russia, Hungary, Moldova, Germany, Slovakia, Belarus and more neighbor countries, these being those that took from a few thousand to millions of immigrants from Ukraine. Most of them being mothers and childrens. The EU has granted Ukrainians the right to stay and work throughout its 27 member nations for up to three years. Refugees are housed in reception centers if they can't stay with friends or relatives. They are given food and medical care, information about onward travel and are entitled to social welfare payments and access to housing, medical treatment and schools.


For some others the situation can be even worse. In some cases, immigrating because of war can lead to lack of access to food, health care, housing, and clean water and sanitation. They also face difficulties in renewing visas, the denial of civil rights and services, the fear of deportation, and anxiety about the future. Precarious living conditions are further heightened by unemployment.


Assimilation

One reason why so many people are against immigrants, legal or ilegal is because they feel their culture and traditions are being threatened by them.


Assimilation is a way in which the process of integrating an ethno cultural, like immigrants, ethic groups, minorities and more, into a bigger or dominating culture, inside what is established common.




It causes rise to racism in societies for it means that people that immigrate to another country with hopes to be treated like a human with rights and respect, (the bare minimum) has to assimilate. They’ll have to leave behind their culture and tradition and sometimes even religion to take up those of the one’s around them.


Of course, they’ll have to begin by the language. Immigrants have always been expected to learn their new country’s language if they hope to fit in. And, yes, one would expect them to learn the country's language someday but until then their rights, living conditions, and opportunities should not depend on that. Not to mention that depending on one's native language, some people still have an accent or a way of pronunciation that will always identify them like outsiders to be targeted.
Multiculturalism

It's the way in which a society deals with the cultural diversity, both at the national and at the community level. Sociologically, Multiculturalism assumes that society as a whole benefits from increased diversity througuh the harmonious coexistence of different cultures. The view that cultures, races, and ethnicities, particullary those of minority groups, deserve special acknowledgement of their differences within a dominant political culture.
